Cava Salad

Ingredients:

  • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup water
  • ¼ cup white vinegar
  • ¼ cup apple cider binegar
  • 1 ½ tsp. honey
  • 1 ½ tsp. salt
  • ¼ tsp. red pepper flakes
  • 1 cup rice
  • 2 chicken breasts
  • Trader Joe's Vegan Creamy Dill Dressing
  • 6 oz. feta, crumbled
  • 1 lemon, juiced
  • ¼ cup oive oil
  • ½ cup pickled jalepenos, diced
  • Honey
  • Cucumbers, chopped
  • Fire roasted red peppers, chopped
  • Salsa
  • Tzatziki
  • Dill
  • Lettuce

Preparation:

  1. Start by making the pickled onions. Pack onions tightly into jar.
  2. In a pan, combine water, both vinegars, honey, salt, and red pepper flakes. Bring to a simmer and then pour over onions.
  3. Use a spoon to press onions into the vinegar and pop any air bubbles. Let onions cool to room temperature for about 30 minutes.
  4. Start cooking the rice in a rice cooker. (If no rice cooker, add 2 cups water to pan and bring to a boil. Add 1 cup rice and allow to simmer. Turn down heat to low and cover. Cook for about 15 minutes and check periodically to see if liquid is fully absorbed.
  5. Start cooking the chicken by lining the air fryer with foil and preheating to 375 degrees.
  6. Add chicken breasts and top with dill dressing, salt, and pepper. Cook for 6 minutes then flip and cook for another 2 to 8 minutes. Chicken temperature should read around 160 degrees.
  7. Next make the feta dip. Add crumbled feta, lemon juice, olive oil, pickled jalepenos, salt, and pepper to a bowl and mash with a fork until creamy. Add a drizzle of honey and mix.
  8. Once rice is cooked, stir in dill and set aside.
  9. Once chicken is cooked and cooled, dice and set aside.
  10. On a large plate or bowl, add lettuce, rice, cucumbers, peppers, salsa, tzatiki, feta dip, chicken, pickled onions and drizzle with dill dressing.


Addi's Notes:

This salad is a bit time consuming but makes up enough to last the week. Once everything is cooked it is easy to assemble. I use whatever lettuce I have on hand, but an herb or arugala mix is the best. I also add whatever veggies I have on hand, normally tomatoes. I like to have this with a side of Naan, drizzled with honey.
